A claim is a request for payment that a healthcare provider submits to a health insurance company.
Prior authorization is required before certain medical procedures or prescriptions are approved.
Claim denial may occur due to missing documents, incorrect patient information, or policy exclusions.
Reimbursement occurs when the insurance plan pays back the member for covered medical expenses.
Coordination of benefits determines which insurance plan pays first when multiple policies exist.
Claim status inquiries allow providers to check whether a claim has been received and processed.
Appeals can be filed when a claim is denied by the insurance company.
Medical billing codes like ICD-10 and CPT are used for claim submission and reimbursement.
Timely filing limits refer to deadlines by which claims must be submitted for payment.
Fraud detection systems analyze claims for duplicate or suspicious billing patterns.
